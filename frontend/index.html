The Longest Race Home

9 Riders, 5 Checkpoints, 360 km minimum, 24 hours, that are the ingredients of The Long Race Home, a cycling challenge for the adventurous.

All riders carry a SPOT gen2 tracker, locations are transmitted with 10 minute intervals. The map and table refresh at 5 minute intervals, so no need to torture F5.

What?

The Long Race Home is a cycling challenge for amateur cyclists. Riders take on the challenge without any support and with minimal guidance: there is a start and finish point and five checkpoints. The timelimit is 24 hours, extendible with 40 minutes if a rider manages to get a picture of himself while drinking Gluhwein in Aachen.

Unsupported means that riders are not allowed to use family or friends to gain help, nor to make arrangements in advance. Drafting is not permitted so all riders have the same chance of reaching the finish line. Checkpoint registration happens with gen2 SPOT trackers and selfregistration by camera. Riders take part on their own responsibility, the TLRH2015 only arranges transport to the start and some service at the finish.

Why?

Because we can?!

Because we don't know whether we can?

Because we never say no to 24 hours of adventure?

Because we like it? (or hope we do)

Because being a dot, might be more interesting than dotwatching?

We do not ride for charity. However we do support Doctors without borders in Belgium and gladly ask those willing to sponsor us to make a small donation here.

The Challenger

TLRH2015 isn't a race, it is a challenge and that is important: in a race there is only one winner, people race against someone. In a challenge riders fight their mind and body, finishing within the timelimit is the goal. Finding food, water and carefully planning the route are all part of the challenge and might prove vital. That being said, when the battle with your mind is over, there is always the question "how did I do?", so there is time registration. And the fastest rider will be declared "winner" of the challenge.

TLRH2015 is a pretty straightforward copy of the worldfamous Transcontinental Race. It's aim is to be more accessible, yet offer a similar level of adventure, albeit in a shorter timespan. For this first event checkpoints were chosen to allow riders to use B-roads or biketrails as the wheather might be extremly bad. This slightly reduces the available navigation options, but was a tradeoff we made to increase safety.
